SECTION 01

Problem Statement

Artificial Intelligence has become a common tool for quickly finding answers, including in the medical domain. Medical students and clinical professionals increasingly rely on AI systems to understand concepts and explore medical knowledge.

However, these systems have a critical limitation: they can generate incorrect information while presenting it confidently, a problem known as hallucination.

In medicine, even small inaccuracies can lead to:

  • Misunderstanding of critical concepts
  • Incorrect learning for students
  • Misinterpretation of clinical knowledge

Since AI usage cannot be avoided, the central research question is: How can AI answers be made more reliable and safe in medical contexts?

SECTION 02

Proposed Solution

MediRAG addresses this challenge by designing a medical question-answering system that prioritizes accuracy and reliability at every stage of the pipeline.

The solution is designed to:

  • Reduce hallucinations
  • Ground responses in reliable medical knowledge
  • Improve overall answer quality

The architecture uses four specialized pipelines for distinct medical domains, including neurosciences, cardiovascular medicine, internal medicine, and mental health with primary care. Each pipeline processes questions independently to improve domain-specific accuracy.
